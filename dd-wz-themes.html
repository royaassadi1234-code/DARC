DD-WZ Semantic Motif Comparison

A chapter-level guide for comparing shared, partial, and text-specific themes between Dādestān ī Dēnīg and Wizīdagīhā ī Zādspram.

Human Purpose and Exaltation

Strong parallel
DD1-6
WZ29-30

Similarity: Both frame the human being as a composite creature with a religious task.

Difference: DD argues through ethical obligation; WZ explains humanity through cosmological composition.

The Nature of Good and Evil

Strong parallel
DD4-5
WZ1-3

Similarity: Both texts distinguish righteous order from destructive opposition.

Difference: DD treats evil through suffering and justice; WZ begins from the mixed cosmic state.

Ahriman and Cosmic Opposition

Strong parallel
DD18, 26, 34-36
WZ1-3, 8, 12

Similarity: Ahriman functions as the principal source of corruption and opposition.

Difference: DD emphasizes eschatological defeat; WZ foregrounds invasion, corruption, and attacks on Zoroaster.

The Advent and Function of Religion

Strong parallel
DD40-41, 47
WZ4, 24-28

Similarity: Religion is the medium through which correct order is known and preserved.

Difference: DD stresses fidelity and performance; WZ narrates religion's arrival and structure.

Zoroaster and Divine Revelation

WZ only
DD-
WZ5-25

Similarity: No direct DD chapter sequence matches WZ's extended prophetic biography.

Difference: WZ makes revelation biographical and narrative; DD uses revealed religion as assumed authority.

The Structure of Religion

Strong parallel
DD44-47
WZ24-28

Similarity: Both connect religious order with teaching, authority, and proper practice.

Difference: DD is institutional and practical; WZ is doctrinal and schematic.

The Soul After Death

Strong parallel
DD18-24
WZ31

Similarity: Both describe the soul's postmortem transition and moral consequence.

Difference: DD distributes the topic across many questions; WZ condenses it into one chapter.

Judgment, Merit, and Sin

Strong parallel
DD7-15
WZ29-31

Similarity: Moral accounting shapes both texts' understanding of human destiny.

Difference: DD gives legal-theological detail; WZ embeds judgment within anthropology and afterlife narrative.

The Chinwad Bridge

Strong parallel
DD20
WZ31

Similarity: The bridge marks the soul's decisive passage after death.

Difference: DD isolates the motif; WZ includes it as part of a larger postmortem sequence.

Heaven and Hell

Strong parallel
DD18-20, 25-26, 30-33
WZ31

Similarity: Both map postmortem destinations to moral condition.

Difference: DD expands heavenly and infernal geography; WZ treats the destinations in compressed narrative form.

The Three Nights After Death

Strong parallel
DD23-24, 27
WZ31

Similarity: Both preserve the three-night interval as a critical threshold.

Difference: DD differentiates ritual and moral implications; WZ folds the interval into the soul's journey.

Vision of Ohrmazd and Ahriman

Strong parallel
DD18
WZ31

Similarity: Both place divine and demonic vision near the afterlife horizon.

Difference: DD raises the vision as a question; WZ integrates it into the soul's encounter.

The Soul's Encounter with Its Deeds

Strong parallel
DD23-24
WZ31

Similarity: Deeds become perceptible and confront the soul after death.

Difference: DD contrasts righteous and wicked souls; WZ presents the encounter as a compact doctrinal scene.

Rituals for the Dead

Partial parallel
DD27, 54, 79-88
WZ31

Similarity: Both connect the dead with ritual care and postmortem outcome.

Difference: DD develops ritual obligation in detail; WZ mainly supplies the afterlife background.

The Corpse and Funerary Practice

Strong parallel
DD14-17
WZ31

Similarity: Death, corpse, and ritual management are tied to purity and judgment.

Difference: DD addresses exposure burial directly; WZ frames death practice through the soul's fate.

Priests and Religious Authority

Strong parallel
DD42-47, 65, 82-87
WZ27

Similarity: Priesthood is central to transmitting and maintaining religion.

Difference: DD examines fees, duties, and authority; WZ highlights priestly virtues and counsel.

Apostasy and Religious Identity

Strong parallel
DD40-41
WZ24-28

Similarity: Both define religious identity through commitment to the good religion.

Difference: DD treats apostasy as an ethical-social danger; WZ defines the religion's content and structure.

Ethics and Virtue

Strong parallel
DD37-41
WZ13-19, 26-27

Similarity: Virtue is presented as active participation in the divine order.

Difference: DD emphasizes saving merit and fidelity; WZ narrates virtues through Zoroaster and religious counsel.

Social Order and Governance

Partial parallel
DD44-52
WZ32

Similarity: Both link social order to religiously grounded responsibility.

Difference: DD turns to markets, contracts, and professions; WZ focuses on professions and bad government.

Inheritance and Family Law

Conceptual parallel
DD53-64
WZ18, 55-61

Similarity: Both can be read through lineage, household continuity, and embodied religious order.

Difference: DD is explicit legal material; the WZ match is conceptual and partly mediated through family/lineage themes.

Creation of Humanity

Strong parallel
DD63-64
WZ29-30

Similarity: Both explain human origin within a larger religious cosmology.

Difference: DD asks about humankind's origin; WZ presents the composition and constitution of humanity.

Cosmology

Strong parallel
DD66-70, 90-92
WZ1-4, 34-35

Similarity: Both relate cosmic structure to religious explanation.

Difference: DD asks about natural phenomena; WZ gives a broader creation-to-renovation arc.

Fate and Free Will

Strong parallel
DD70
WZ1-4

Similarity: Both imply tension between cosmic conditions and moral action.

Difference: DD addresses fate and lunar influence directly; WZ embeds agency within the cosmic conflict.

Resurrection (Ristāxēz)

Strong parallel
DD34-36
WZ34-35

Similarity: Both place resurrection in the final defeat of evil.

Difference: DD discusses depopulation, agents, and measure; WZ presents resurrection inside the renovation sequence.

The Final Renovation (Frašgird)

Strong parallel
DD35-36
WZ34-35

Similarity: Both envision cosmic restoration as the culmination of history.

Difference: DD foregrounds agents and salvific merit; WZ emphasizes the ordered renewal of the world.

The Role of the Amesha Spentas

Strong parallel
DD21, 23, 35-36
WZ23, 35

Similarity: The divine agents support cosmic order and final restoration.

Difference: DD invokes them across afterlife and renovation motifs; WZ associates them with interviews and cosmic order.

The Sevenfold Cosmic Order

Strong parallel
DD35-36
WZ23, 35

Similarity: Both connect ordered plurality with renovation and divine structure.

Difference: DD ties the order to final events; WZ uses it in revelatory and renovative frameworks.

Natural Phenomena

DD only
DD66-70, 90-92
WZ-

Similarity: WZ has cosmology, but no direct matched chapter set for these natural questions.

Difference: DD preserves discrete explanations of rainbow, moon, eclipses, earthquakes, waters, and rainfall.

Sexual Ethics and Purity

Partial parallel
DD71-78
WZ18

Similarity: Both can be connected through purity, embodied order, and family structure.

Difference: DD explicitly treats sexual sin and punishment; WZ 18 is only a partial thematic contact.
