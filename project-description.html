Project description

Tracing the semantic ambiguity of evil in Middle Persian textual traditions.

In the Avesta, Druz and Angra Mainyu appear as distinct principles of evil. In ninth-century Pahlavi literature, however, their semantic boundaries often become less stable and at times appear to overlap.

This project investigates that ambiguity through a comparative study of the Dādestān ī Dēnīg and the Wizīdagīhā ī Zādspram, combining close linguistic analysis with digital humanities methods for searching, comparison, and annotation.
