Digital Humanities Project

Researching Iranian textual traditions through philology and digital methods

This project studies how evil principles such as druz and Ahreman are configured across selected Middle Persian materials from ninth-century Iran, especially Dādestān ī Dēnīg and Wizīdagīhā ī Zādspram.

The workspace brings structured search, comparison, annotation, translation, and visualization methods to ancient Iranian sources so that philological detail and larger conceptual patterns can be read together.
