DD

Dādestān ī Dēnīg

A 9th-century Pahlavi work by Manuščihr, high priest of the Persian Zoroastrian community, organized as religious questions and answers on doctrinal, legal, ethical, and cosmological matters.

WZ

Wizīdagīhā ī Zādspram

The Anthology of Zādspram, a 9th-century Zoroastrian scholar, preserves theological and exegetical material, including traditions connected with lost Avestan Zand texts.

PY

Pahlavi Yasna

The Pahlavi Yasna the Middle Persian (Pahlavi) translation and commentary (Zand) of the primary Zoroastrian liturgical text and central ritual (the Yasna), originally written in Avestan. It provides crucial insights into how ancient Zoroastrian priests understood, interpreted, and translated their sacred texts.

NM

Nāmagīhā ī Manušcihr

Manuščihr, son of Juwān-Jam, was the Commander of the Profession of Priests in Fārs and Kerman and the author of three epistles, written in 881 CE.

See more

One was addressed to the authorities of Sīrgān, one to his younger brother, Zādspram, and one, a public decree, condemned Zādspram’s ruling that allowed a simplified purification ritual instead of Baršnūm (BARAŠNOM) in case of contact with the dead.

Digital Humanities Project

Researching Iranian textual traditions through philology and digital methods

This project studies how evil principles such as druz and Ahreman are configured across selected Middle Persian materials from ninth-century Iran, especially Dādestān ī Dēnīg and Wizīdagīhā ī Zādspram.

The workspace brings structured search, comparison, annotation, translation, and visualization methods to ancient Iranian sources so that philological detail and larger conceptual patterns can be read together.
